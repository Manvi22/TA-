Talent Acquisition Tournament — Master Video Prompt

Use this as a single, comprehensive prompt for an AI video generator (Sora, Runway, Pika, Veo, etc.) or as a creative brief for a designer/animator.


1. Concept Summary

Create a 10–12 second animated sports-style race video titled "Talent Acquisition Tournament." Five cartoon characters, each representing a regional Circle Head in an HR organization, race down a stadium running track. The video should feel like a fun, high-energy internal company celebration — playful and competitive, never mean-spirited. It must end with an unambiguous Champion announcement followed by a full final ranking (1st–5th place) displayed clearly on screen.


2. Characters (Cartoon, Stylized, Office-Themed)

Each character is a friendly cartoon avatar of a businessperson (rounded features, simple expressive face, professional attire — blazer or shirt), running in an athletic but office-appropriate way (carrying a subtle prop like a folder or ID badge flapping behind them is a nice touch). Each has a colored circular badge beneath them showing their region.

3. Visual Theme & Style


Setting: A floodlit night-time stadium running track, viewed from a side-on / broadcast camera angle — five parallel lanes on a red-and-maroon athletic track surface with white dashed lane dividers.
Atmosphere: Sweeping stadium floodlight beams, a subtle haze/glow, an excited but unseen crowd (audio cue only — no crowd shown).
Color palette: Deep charcoal/black background, brick-red track lanes, gold (#FFC857) accent for trophy/champion moments, cyan (#2BE0C6) for energy/commentary highlights, with each runner's individual accent color as listed above.
Typography (for any on-screen text/titles): Bold condensed sports/stadium-style display font (e.g., Anton or similar) for headlines and the scoreboard; a digital/LED monospace font (e.g., Share Tech Mono) for timers and the final ranking board, evoking an airport split-flap or stadium scoreboard.
No course/checkpoint labels along the track — keep the track visually clean, just lanes and runners.
Style reference: Think "corporate sports day meets stadium broadcast" — polished, energetic, a little tongue-in-cheek, but always respectful and positive in tone.



4. Shot-by-Shot Sequence


Title Card (0:00–0:02): "TALENT ACQUISITION TOURNAMENT" appears in bold stadium lettering with a gold glow, floodlights sweeping behind it. Subtitle: "5 Circle Heads. One track. Only one lifts the trophy."
Starting Line (0:02–0:04): All five characters shown at the starting blocks in their lanes, each labeled with name + region badge. A stadium-style countdown overlay plays: "3… 2… 1… GO!" with a flash of light on "GO!"
The Race (0:04–0:09): All five characters sprint down the track with energetic running animation (slight bob, motion trail/blur behind each runner). Positions shuffle realistically — there should be visible lead changes and a tight pack mid-race for tension — but the final arrival order must match the table above. Add a moving on-screen commentary ticker/caption bar with short, upbeat, generic phrases (see Section 5).
Photo Finish (0:09–0:10): Mr. Anand crosses a checkered finish line tape first, breaking it dramatically, followed in sequence by the other four in their designated order, each crossing with a small individual flourish.
Champion Reveal (0:10–0:11): Screen transitions to a celebratory banner: a large trophy icon bounces in, with text "TOURNAMENT CHAMPION — Mr. Anand — Gujarat & East Circle Head," gold confetti bursts from both sides of the frame.
Final Ranking Scoreboard (0:11–0:12+): A scoreboard panel flips in row by row (like an airport split-flap display), clearly listing all 5 positions:

Mr. Anand — Gujarat & East — 🥇
Mr. Bhavani — South — 🥈
Mr. Akhil — North — 🥉
Mr. Amit — Rajasthan & MPCG
Mr. Ashish — Maharashtra
Each row animates in with a slight delay for a satisfying cascading reveal, ending on a clean, fully readable final frame that holds for at least 2 seconds.






5. Commentary / Caption Tone

All on-screen captions and any voiceover should be playful, energetic, and entirely good-natured — never teasing or implying anyone underperformed. Example lines:


"And they're off! All five Circle Heads charging ahead!"
"Great energy across every lane — full throttle!"
"It's a tight pack as the tournament heats up!"
"The race is wide open — anyone's tournament to win!"
"Final stretch! Everyone giving it their absolute best!"
"Mr. Anand breaks the tape first — Gujarat & East takes the win!"
"Fantastic finish from every single Circle Head today!"


Avoid any line that singles someone out negatively (no jokes about being slow, last place, mistakes, etc.) — keep the humor centered on the excitement of the race itself, not on any individual.


6. Audio Suggestions (optional, if generator supports sound)


Upbeat, light orchestral/electronic sports-arena track (think Olympic broadcast intro energy, not aggressive).
Crowd cheer swell building toward the finish line.
A short triumphant fanfare sting (1–2 seconds) on the Champion Reveal.
Soft "whoosh" sounds on each runner crossing the finish line.



7. Technical Specs


Duration: 10–15 seconds total (loopable ending optional).
Aspect ratio: 16:9 for desktop/screen sharing, or provide a 9:16 vertical cut for mobile/WhatsApp sharing.
Resolution: 1080p minimum.
Frame rate: 24–30 fps, smooth easing on all motion (no jittery animation).
End state: The final frame must hold on the complete, legible ranking list — this is non-negotiable, as the clear outcome is the entire point of the video.
